Client Report

How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?

I wanted something that wasn't too confusing to build. I tweaked my original idea and built out a wireframe, based on a different website.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?

We communicated over email. There wasn't much to communicate about, but I did manage to send my wireframe to the wrong person, so that didn't help.

Doesn't look like their junior devs did anything. The homepage looks good though! The only issues are my fault, the svg didn't scale like I expected it to.

I'll check up more often.

Lead Developer Report

How did you manage your development team and communicate the tasks for your junior devs?

I made GitHub Issues and assigned them to people. I don't think that worked, I should've used more direct communication alongside it, like email.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?

We communicated over email and occasionally in class. It was pretty smooth, and he came up with what I needed soon after I asked him for it. I got mixed up since he's my senior for another project, but it worked out.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?

I used GitHub pages and shared the published link with them.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?

It was pretty good. I'm used to using Git, so it worked pretty naturally. No merge conflicts that I know of.

General Experience and Reflection

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?

The hardest part was working with unclear or missing information. It was easy to overcome with Flannery, he supplied info when I asked. For Tuft, I kinda just did my best. No issues with Bentley.

What did you enjoy most about this project?

It was nice to not have to worry about making things look nice after I did the wireframe. I just needed to copy what I was given. That probably would get boring fast though.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?

I'd probably make sure I communicate with my juniors better. I kinda just assumed they'd figure it out based on the issues I assigned, and they'd ask me otherwise. That's an oops on my part.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.

A little bit. I know a bit more about how working on different timelines works, and what to look out for when dealing with clients. Nobody got mad since it's a school class, but I imagine there would be problems in the real world.
